About Me

I'm an undergraduate student who's seriously focused on becoming a strong software developer through consistent, hands-on work. I didn't get into tech just to watch tutorials — I learn by building. Most of my time goes into creating real projects, understanding how systems work behind the scenes, and improving step by step. I care more about practical skills than hype, and I treat every project as a chance to level up.

My main interests are backend development, full-stack web applications, and writing clean, logical code. I work with technologies like Python, Django, Java, and modern web tools such as HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I enjoy connecting frontend and backend to turn ideas into working products. Along the way, I focus on fundamentals, problem-solving, and writing code that's simple, readable, and actually useful.

What drives me is growth. I'm always looking at what I built yesterday and thinking, "How can I do this better?" I like challenging myself with tougher projects, learning from mistakes, and improving consistently rather than chasing shortcuts. My goal is to become an engineer who can build reliable, real-world software — and this portfolio is a record of that journey.
